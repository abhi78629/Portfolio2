What is this project?

This project addresses the critical need for early diabetes detection through predictive modeling. By leveraging ensemble learning methods, specifically the Random Forest algorithm, we achieved high predictive accuracy while maintaining clinical interpretability. The model analyzes physiological markers to provide risk assessments.

Quantifying the contribution of each physiological marker to the prediction.

Outlier Capping

IQR-based capping to prevent extreme biometric values from skewing model splits.

SMOTE

Key Lessons & Scientific Insights

  • Ensemble over Individual: Random Forest consistently outperformed single decision trees by mitigating overfitting.
  • Depth Control: Finding the optimal tree depth is crucial—too deep led to noise capture, too shallow led to underfitting.
  • Interpretability: For clinical applications, the 'Gini importance' metrics were as valuable as the prediction itself.
  • Data Quality: IQR-based outlier capping significantly stabilized the forest variance during cross-validation.

Researcher's Note

"Transitioning from Logistic Regression to Random Forest revealed that non-linear relationships between glucose, insulin, and age were the missing link in reaching >94% accuracy. The sheer power of ensemble methods lies in their ability to uncover these complex interactions."
